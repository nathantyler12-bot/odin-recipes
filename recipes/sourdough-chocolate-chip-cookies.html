Sourdough Chocolate Chip Cookies

Sourdough chocolate chip cookies from Nathan's kitchen. Look at all those chocolate chips!

These chocolate chip cookies are absolutely wonderful. Large, lots of chocolate, and best of all it uses up your sourdough starter discard. So, yes, this recipe will be difficult to do without some starter, but someone you know might have some! Or head to your local bakery to see if they have any discard. Anyway, the discard adds a lovely tang to the cookie and gives you the opportunity to brown the butter to get more moisture out (that's needed since all the water content will be coming from your discard). Browning the butter adds a deep, rich brown color to the cookies as well as a nutty flavor that is noticeable all through the cookie (and your kitchen - you'll thank me later!)

All in all, this recipe probably doesn't differ too much from your basic Nestle Toll House recipe ingredient wise. Almost all the ratios are the same, the ingredients are just transformed a bit - like some flour and water content being in the sourdough starter discard and the butter being browned to remove some water content. The biggest recipe change is probably in the two sugars. Most chocolate chip cookie recipes have an even 50/50 split between granulated and brown sugar. This recipe is 60/40 in favor of brown sugar. It gives it a more rich molasses flavor. If you can't get a hold of discard, just do the Toll House recipe and adjust that ratio.

Yields 25 cookies

Prep Time: 50 minutes, plus 4-24 hours for chilling. Bake time: 18 minutes plus 10 minutes cooling

Ingredients

These will all be in grams (mostly). Weigh your ingredients versus using volume. Trust me.

  • 225g unsalted butter (diced)
  • 380g flour
  • 1tsp baking powder
  • 1tsp baking soda
  • 1tsp salt
  • 176g sugar
  • 264g light brown sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 240g sourdough starter discard
  • 1tsp vanilla extract
  • 500g chocolate chips

Steps

Prepare the dough

  1. Get all of your bowls, untensils, and ingredients together and measured.
  2. Brown the butter over medium high heat. Cook until it smells nutty and looks deep brown. Let cool 30 minutes.
  3. In a medium bowl, whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  4. In a different, large bowl and using electric mixer, mix brown sugar, sugar, and butter until large lumps are gone.
  5. Add egg yolks to sugar and butter mixture and mix on medium-high for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add starter discard and vanilla to butter mixture. Mix until smooth.
  7. Add in the flour mixture and mix on low until it all comes together.
  8. Mix in chocolate chips.
  9. Refrigerate dough for 4-24 hours.

Bake the dough

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Roll dough into balls of 70g each. Add 6 to a normal size baking sheet to space out cookies well.
  3. Bake 18 minutes. If puffy when coming out, firmly tap the baking sheet on the counter.
  4. Cool for 10 minutes.

The dough freezes pretty well. So if you're not planning on baking all 25 at once, roll out the rest of the dough into balls, cover, and then flash freeze overnight. Store in a plastic bad. Bake directly from freezing by baking at 350°F for 20 minutes.
